---
title: "Juniper Contamination"
layout: "single"
---

In June 2022, I received a 50kg juniper lot from a Vietnam-based botanical supplier and entered it into production without any quality gate at receipt. The lot was used across four consecutive batches between July and August 2022, spanning three products. All four batches passed post-production quality control (QC) — the contamination was real but the protocol had no mechanism to detect gradual flavour drift across consecutive batches. Detection came 56 days after the lot was received, through an unplanned batch comparison during a routine tasting session. By then, 715 bottles had been filled and distributed. The blast radius was not determined by the severity of the failure. It was determined by how long the detection gap allowed it to run.

The investigation confirmed the contamination was isolated to a single lot. I quarantined all four batches on the same day the root cause was confirmed. I did not act on initial suspicion. I acted on confirmed evidence. The structural fix was mandatory lot-level sensory testing at ingredient receipt, moving detection upstream of production entirely.

---

**Detection latency:** 56 days from receipt to confirmation.

**Blast radius:** 4 batches, 3 products, 715 bottles quarantined.

**What changed:** Lot-level sensory validation introduced at ingredient receipt. Detection moved upstream of production.
