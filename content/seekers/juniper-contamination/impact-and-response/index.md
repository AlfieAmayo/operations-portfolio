## Impact

Confirmation on 15 August 2022 triggered an immediate blast radius assessment. Four batches produced between 11 July and 8 August were affected, spanning three products: Seekers Mekong Gold Gin, Seekers Mekong Dry Gin, and Jason Kong Butterfly Pea Gin. Across those four batches, 715 bottles were quarantined.

By the time the issue was confirmed, affected stock had already been distributed to both on-trade and off-trade accounts. Once stock left the warehouse, customer exposure could not be avoided, so recovery was prioritised by account volume, with highest-volume accounts contacted first. Approximately 70% of affected stock was recovered, while the remaining 30% was likely consumed before collection was possible. No stock had shipped internationally, so the contamination was contained within the domestic Cambodia market.

## Response

On the day of confirmation, stock recovery was initiated immediately. The supplier was notified, and the remaining juniper stock of lot PDI-JNP-220620 was removed from production storage.

A replacement juniper lot from the same supplier was already in stock, so production continued without interruption. Before use, I validated it through single botanical distillation against the documented house baseline, the same method used to identify the issue. The profile was clean and consistent with expectations, with juniper expression back to the expected intensity and character. QC rated the lot Very Good. Production resumed the same day, with the replacement lot approved before the first post-incident batch.
