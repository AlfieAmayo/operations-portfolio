## Impact

Confirmation on 15 August 2022 that the juniper lot was the source of the deviation triggered an immediate blast radius assessment. Four batches produced between 11 July and 8 August were affected, spanning three products: Seekers Mekong Gold Gin, Seekers Mekong Dry Gin, and Jason Kong Butterfly Pea Gin. Across those four batches, 715 bottles were quarantined.

By the time the issue was confirmed, affected stock had already been distributed to both on-trade and off-trade accounts. Once stock had left the warehouse, customer exposure could not be prevented, so recovery was prioritised by account volume, with the highest-volume accounts contacted first. Approximately 70% of affected stock was recovered. The remaining 30% was likely consumed before collection. No stock had shipped internationally, so the contamination was contained within the Cambodian market.

## Response

Stock recovery began immediately on confirmation. The supplier was notified, and the remaining juniper stock from lot PDI-JNP-220620 was removed from production storage.

A replacement juniper lot from the same supplier was already in stock, so production did not stop. Before use, I validated the replacement lot through single botanical distillation against the documented house baseline, using the same method that identified the issue. The distillation profile was clean and consistent with expectations. On that basis, the replacement lot was approved for production, and production resumed the same day. 

Quality control on the first post-contamination gin batch confirmed that the botanical profile had returned to house standard.
