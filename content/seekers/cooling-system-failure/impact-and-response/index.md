title: "Impact and Response"
---

## Affected Batch

One batch was affected. Batch 50, Seekers Mekong Dry Gin, produced on 29 May 2023 on the 2000L still, failed QC during routine assessment and was quarantined. It did not ship. Post-incident analysis confirmed temperature drift of 1 to 2°C per hour, accumulating to approximately 24°C across the 12-hour run.

## Blast Radius

At containment, one batch had been produced against a planned run of five. Without intervention, batches 2 through 5 would have run on the same system, with the same temperature drift. Each would have added compromised volume to the shipment. The flavour deviation would not have been detectable during the run and would only surface at post-production QC, at least two weeks later. Continuing to produce under the same system would have consumed both raw materials and shipment time, leaving no viable path to recover before shipment.

Containment reduced the blast radius from five batches to one.

The blast radius was determined by when detection occurred relative to the production schedule. The outcome would have been the same. Only the number of compromised batches would have increased.

## Containment Decision

At 10:30 AM on 12 June, the deviation had been confirmed as real and batch-wide, but the root cause remained unknown. Batch 50 was resting in tanks. Botanicals for batch 2 had been staged and weighed but not yet committed. No bottling, labelling, or outbound movement had occurred. Customer exposure was zero. No irreversible commitments had been made.

The decision to contain was made without a confirmed root cause. Pausing carried a bounded cost: one batch lost and less time to recover. Continuing carried an unbounded cost: each additional run added compromised volume, with no guarantee the issue would be identified before it repeated.

I locked the batch 50 tanks, halted batch 2 preparation, and discarded the staged botanicals due to their short usability window. Exposure was capped at one batch at the earliest point intervention was still possible.

## Reversibility

All downstream steps were still reversible at the point of containment. No product had been bottled, labelled, or moved. Exposure remained zero. The only losses were one batch and the discarded botanicals from batch 2.

Production restarted on Mae, the 300L legacy still, while the root cause of the 2000L failure was investigated.
