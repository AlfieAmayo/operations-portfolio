## Before the Run

Two runs preceded Batch 50 and shaped how I interpreted the outcome. Batches 48 and 49 were both Orange Liqueur, each running for 7.5 hours and passing quality control (QC) without issue.

During those runs, I observed that the 2000L still ran hotter than the 300L legacy still, with distillate output temperature progressing from cool to warm to hot. The observation was tactile, not instrumented. I noted it and did not return to review it because both batches passed QC and matched the expected flavour. Those two runs became the reference point for how I interpreted Batch 50. They did not reveal the failure mode. Nothing in those runs suggested there was a problem. When the same pattern appeared during Batch 50, I treated it as normal because I had seen it twice without consequence.

## The Run

Batch 50, Seekers Mekong Dry Gin, ran on 29 May 2023 for 12 hours.

The dashboard was checked during the run, but the elevated temperature was not treated as something that required action. No threshold had been defined, and the same temperature increase had already been observed during the Orange Liqueur runs, where it had no impact on quality. Instead, visible condensation was treated as confirmation that the system was operating correctly.

At the end of the run, the output felt unusually hot, and I noted a temperature reading of 61.8°C on the display. The reading was elevated, but its significance was not understood, so the batch was set to rest pending QC. That value is the only temperature record from the run.

## Detection

On 12 June 2023, 14 days after production, I tasted Batch 50 during routine QC. The gin did not match the expected standard. I could taste a difference, but I wanted to rule out individual bias, so I asked the production team to taste it independently. They confirmed the same issue, and the batch failed QC.

By 10:30 AM, botanicals for Batch 51 had been prepared but not yet added to the still. I locked the Batch 50 tanks to prevent any downstream movement and halted preparation before the botanicals were added. Customer exposure was still zero.

The 14 day gap between production and detection was not a process failure. QC ran on schedule. The failure was that there was no actionable signal during the run.
