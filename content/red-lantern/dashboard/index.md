title: "Supply Chain Dashboard"
---

Red Lantern Dining operated a 14-site franchise network. I ran a centralised RTD beverage programme for two drinks: Ginger Lime Sparkling and Lychee Yuzu Soda. Across 18 orders, the programme reduced GLS raw material cost by 29.80% and cut lead time from seven weeks to three.

In the initial phase, I used a shared Excel file to coordinate across all production nodes while the programme was still being iterated and stabilised. Each partner updated their part of the file directly, which was the simplest and most reliable way to keep everyone aligned. This worked well for execution and speed, but visibility only came through updates, not continuously, so issues usually showed up once they hit a downstream step.

This dashboard reconstructs that history with the visibility the Excel tracker did not provide. OTIF is the primary delivery metric. COGS is shown order by order, with each inflection point linked to a decision. Supplier performance is attributed by responsible party, not just overall score. It makes tradeoffs between cost, reliability, and supplier choice explicit.

A query panel provides direct access to five operational questions: which interventions improved performance, which deliveries succeeded by absorbing a problem, where reliability was chosen over margin, what the stable baseline looks like, and three forward-looking recommendations for Factory B capacity, Factory A labelling, and LYS margin erosion.

The data covers Apex Beverage (Factory A), Crest Bottling (Factory B), and seven suppliers. All figures are real. Client and supplier names are fictionalised.
